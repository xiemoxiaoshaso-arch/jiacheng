The much-anticipated EU New Battery Regulation (Battery Passport) related details have officially entered the mandatory execution stage. This marks a historic upgrade in the overseas entry and low-carbon compliance standards for all industrial and energy storage batteries entering the EU and European Economic Area markets.

### 1. What is the "Battery Passport"?

The new regulation mandates that industrial batteries and energy storage batteries with a capacity of over 2kWh must have a digitally scannable "Battery Passport". It requires exporting companies to fully disclose the following core metadata in the digital ID:

*   **Raw Material Traceability**: Strictly trace the supply chain compliance and human rights protection of core active minerals such as lithium, cobalt, and nickel.
*   **Carbon Footprint Assessment**: Publicly disclose the total carbon dioxide emissions throughout the product's entire life cycle.
*   **Recycled Material Proportion**: Specify the actual utilization percentage of recycled raw materials like copper, cobalt, and lithium in the battery.
